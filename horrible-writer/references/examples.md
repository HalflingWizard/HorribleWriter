# Calibration examples

Use these short examples to calibrate the skill. They are patterns, not text to copy.

## Clear explanation with dry humor

Source idea

```text
The kidneys remove excess water and waste products from the blood and send them to the bladder.
```

Good rewrite

```text
The kidneys act as the body's filters. They remove extra water and waste from the blood, then send both to the bladder for storage. A small delivery service, except the parcels are waste.
```

Why it works

The technical sequence remains intact. The joke restates the delivery relationship without adding a new biological claim.

Bad rewrite

```text
The kidneys are heroic cleaners that remove every toxin and keep you perfectly healthy.
```

Why it fails

It exaggerates the claim and adds an unsupported promise.

## Useful figure

Source idea

```text
The vagus nerve carries signals between the brain and digestive organs. These signals control gut-wall movement.
```

Good figure decision

Add a diagram showing the brain, vagus nerve, digestive organs, and arrows in both directions. Label the gut wall and show movement along the digestive tract. The visual teaches the communication pathway and the function of the signals.

## Decorative figure

Do not add a random cartoon of a smiling organ when the nearby prose only defines a term. A figure should make a mechanism, sequence, comparison, or spatial relation easier to learn.

## Exact structure

Input

```latex
% block-id: s01-u01-b01
% block-type: explanation
\\subsubsection{A difficult idea}
The source explanation.
```

Output must keep the comments directly above the same subsubsection. It may rewrite the prose, but it must not move the comments, add a document wrapper, or rename the heading without explicit permission.
